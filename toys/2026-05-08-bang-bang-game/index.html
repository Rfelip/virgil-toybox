What is bang-bang control?

Optimal control asks: given a system you can influence, what is the best strategy over time? In fishery management, that means deciding when and how hard to harvest.

Pontryagin's Maximum Principle tells us that under many conditions, the best strategy is bang-bang: push the control to its extreme (fish hard or don't fish at all). There is no advantage to half-measures—or is there?

These three puzzles explore when bang-bang wins, when it can be beaten by a singular arc (steady middle effort), and how switching dynamics shape the optimal policy. Explore stochastic variants and deeper theory in the research notes.
