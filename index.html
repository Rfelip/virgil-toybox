Sound of Primes

number-theoretic sequences as music

Twin primes, Goldbach pairs, the Sieve of Eratosthenes, Mersenne bells, Collatz trajectories — eight modes. Primes sing sine; composites buzz square; the sieve clicks and rings its way through a grid of numbers. Pentatonic C major, C3–A5.

Library of Babel

a small infinite library you can wander

Every page has an address. Navigate by coordinate, search for any phrase (the library places it rather than finds it), visit curated galleries, bookmark pages to a corkboard. The librarian speaks from the margin, sparsely. Borges’ six axioms in the sidebar; citations to the canonical Basile version.

Sound of Sorting

nine algorithms, made audible

Each comparison and write fires a soft sine pluck mapped to a pentatonic scale. Bubble sort: a slow ascending wash. Radix sort: sweeping mechanical waves. Pancake sort: theatrical stack-flips. Plus cocktail shaker, stooge, and four more. Watch the bars and listen.

L-systems Clock

an hour is a shape; a minute, a depth

Eight Lindenmayer systems cycle through the day — plant, fern, bush, Koch, Sierpinski, dragon, wave, leaf. The hour picks the shape and rotates its seed angle; the minute drives iteration depth. Glance at the figure and you know what time it is, no digits required. Pure stdlib Python, runs in any terminal.

Prose Bichromator

two languages in every English sentence

Paste English prose, see each word painted by its etymological lineage — Germanic in burnt sienna, Latinate in lapis blue. Bureaucratic prose lights up cool; lyric prose stays warm. The register a writer chooses becomes visible at a glance.

Twenty-five hand-curated cards across measure theory's exam-frequent core — σ-algebras, MCT/DCT/Fatou, Lp, Radon–Nikodym, Fubini.
